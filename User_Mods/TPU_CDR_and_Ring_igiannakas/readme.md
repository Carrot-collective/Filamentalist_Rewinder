# TPU CDR "O-ring"

This modification aims to address one area of potential wear with the Filamentalist - the O-rings in the CDR assembly.

The standard Buna-N/NBR O-rings offer plenty of traction; however, over time they tend to flake and deposit rubber dust on filament. This is particularly evident on white, friction-abrasive filament, such as matte white PLA, especially when printing multi-k tool swap prints.

As the rubber material is not meltable in the toolhead it tends to extrude with the filament and appear like black dust specks on the print.

This modification aims to address this wear item.

![IMG_8320](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fd2f6683-88df-4891-b53f-4214914823bb)

## Why TPU?

TPU is extremely abrasion resistant, hence resists wear and reduces the creation of dust particles. In addition, as TPU is a thermoplastic, it melts with the filament and mixes in the nozzle. Therefore, even if any particles are created, they will not be visible in the print as they will be molten in the extruded material.

## TPU selection:

White or transparent TPU is recommended, as both can mix with any filament color without causing any visible contamination. 

90A and 95A TPU has been tested as working. Lower hardness TPU tends to have higher traction, so they are preferable.

## Print settings:

**For the TPU ring**
- 0.2 layer height
- 6 walls (all walls, no infill in the slicer preview)
- Random seam to avoid excessive material accumulation in a single zone

**For the CDR**
- Follow the Filamentalist print instructions

## Calibration and setup:

Slightly higher spring tension may be necessary as the TPU has less grip than the rubber in the O-rings.
